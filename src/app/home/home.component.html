The Amsterdam Good Murder Guide

'This is the story of how I became a serial killer. And I know that sounds bad — you hear “serial killer” and immediately you think: “bad egg”. Well … not this time. I'm the exception that proves the rule. I'm a good guy, and the nicest multiple murderer in history. You'll see…'

So begins the increasingly incendiary confession of Amsterdam tour guide, female supremacist and confused Buddhist, Wesley Bell. After seven years of non-violence, Wesley's dream of a world without sociopaths is given a boost when, entirely incidentally, he's caught up in a pair of fortuitous freak fatalities. Then when yet more toxic humans cross his path, Wesley is faced with a choice: does he sit back and allow these soulless trolls to wreak their terrible havoc? Or does he take arms against a sea of misogynistic man-demons, and strike a blow for the greater good?

Bête De Jour: The Intimate Adventures of an Ugly Man

After an eventful school reunion and the rekindling of an old friendship, self-confessed ugly fellow Stan Cattermole turns 30 and gives himself a year to lose half his body weight and find himself someone to love, becoming something of an internet celebrity in the process.

I'll be honest, I just dipped into my last remaining copy of this book to check something, and poof! —  an hour was gone. I'm sorry if this sounds arrogant, but this book is bloody magnificent. I'd honestly forgotten how good it is. Shame so few people read it. Ricky Gervais almost optioned it apparently. But then he didn't. Doesn't mean it isn't great. It is.

It's a novel by the way, that for complicated reasons, was published by Collins as an autobiography. Life is weird. This book is genuinely awesome. Buy it now in print.

The Lives and Loves of Hana Lee

Concert pianist, nun, sex worker, spy, Hana Lee is also, by any definition, a serial killer. Even if most of her victims did have it coming, all that killing was bound to take its toll, so in 2012, Hana Lee is in London looking for an exit strategy. All she has to do before she goes is make a full confession. Which is when she meets Lucia, and everything changes…

Part contemporary love story, part historical biography, The Lives and Loves of Hana Lee is an extraordinary story of female love, rage and empowerment, set against a backdrop of two hundred years of male chauvinism. By turns explicit, shocking, darkly funny and deeply moving.
